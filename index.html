01. Service & Importance

What is Customer Service?

Professional assistance, care, and attention provided before, during, and after a salon service. It combines technical skills, communication, and ethics.

Importance of Quality Service

  • Builds trust and professional reputation.
  • Encourages critical repeat business.
  • Supports salon growth and profitability.
  • Reduces customer complaints and conflicts.

Milady Connection

Customer retention depends just as much on the quality of the service experience as it does on technical results.

02. Understanding Customers

Internal Customers

Individuals inside the organization (supervisors, managers, receptionists, and other cosmetologists) who rely on cooperation to do their jobs.

External Customers

Individuals outside the organization (new, regular, walk-in, or scheduled clients) receiving salon services.

Special Needs Customers

Treat all individuals affected by language barriers, disabilities, cultural or religious beliefs, age, or gender with dignity, fairness, and respect.

03. Language Barriers

Associated Challenges

  • Misunderstanding of service options.
  • Incorrect product usage or safety risks.
  • Client dissatisfaction and stress.

Professional Strategies

  • Use simple, clear language without technical jargon.
  • Employ clear physical demonstrations and visual aids.
  • Confirm understanding and seek assistance when needed.
Page 3

04. Communication Skills

Verbal & Non-Verbal Skills

  • Verbal: Clear, polite language, accurate explanations, and professional tone.
  • Non-Verbal: Eye contact, positive body language, and professional grooming.
  • Active Listening: Listen without interrupting, clarify details, and respond appropriately.

Building Rapport

Rapport is a positive, trusting relationship. Build it by showing genuine interest, respecting boundaries, and being courteous.

05. The Client Journey

Step 1: Before the Service

Greet the customer politely, confirm their needs and expectations, and prepare a fully sanitized workstation.

Step 2: During the Service

Explain procedures step-by-step, check client comfort levels regularly, and strictly follow safety rules.

Step 3: After the Service

Confirm client satisfaction, provide professional aftercare advice, and thank the customer warmly.

06. Challenging Clients

The Picky / Perfectionist Client

Behavior: Highly specific expectations; notices tiny details.

Response: Listen carefully, clarify details before starting, confirm steps throughout, and avoid defensiveness.

The Conservative / Reserved Client

Behavior: Quiet, cautious, uncomfortable with sudden changes.

Response: Use gentle communication, respect preferences, avoid pressure, and offer reassurance.

Page 6

07. Challenging Clients (Cont.)

The Talkative Client

Behavior: Enjoys chatting; may distract you from your work.

Response: Remain polite, gently steer focus back to the service, avoid oversharing, and manage your time.

The Dissatisfied Client

Behavior: Expresses anger or disappointment over service results.

Response: Stay calm and respectful, listen without interrupting, apologize sincerely, and offer solutions within salon policies.

Page 7

08. Complaints & Feedback

Handling Salon Complaints

View complaints as opportunities to learn. Practice sensitivity, courtesy, and active problem-solving, and document according to salon guidelines.

Monitoring Satisfaction

Track quality by observing client reactions, asking for feedback directly, and watching client return rates.

Feedback Methods

Gather praise, suggestions, or reviews via verbal communication, satisfaction surveys, or service records.

Page 8

09. Records & Ethics

Maintaining Customer Records

Use client files to document services, preferred products and styling formulas, allergies, and feedback — always ethically.

Ethics & Safety Responsibilities

  • Maintain excellent sanitation standards.
  • Protect client physical and chemical safety.
  • Adhere to salon privacy and confidentiality.
  • Ensure non-discriminatory service always.
